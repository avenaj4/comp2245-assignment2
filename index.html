Designing With Intention: My UI Workflow

October 1st, 2025 by Avena Joseph

Whenever I start working on a new design, I always grab a notebook first. I like scribbling messy sketches before touching any software it helps me figure out what I actually want to build. Once I see the ideas on paper, I move into simple wireframes on the computer. That way I can test the flow and layout before worrying about colors and fonts.

After that, I focus on making everything feel consistent. I play with spacing, colors, and typography until the design feels balanced. I try to keep accessibility in mind too like checking contrast and making sure text is easy to read. By the time I add the final polish, the design already feels functional and smooth.

From Idea to Deployment: Shipping My First React App

September 15, 2025 by Avena Joseph

I finally built and launched my first React app this month! It’s a simple tracker that helps me log study sessions and see how much time I spend on each subject. At first, I was nervous about breaking things, but keeping the app small really helped me focus. I started with just the basics adding, editing, and viewing sessions.

The most exciting part was putting it online. I set up a basic workflow that builds and tests my code whenever I push changes, and then it automatically deploys. Seeing my app live on the web felt amazing. It’s definitely not perfect, but I learned a lot about React, Git, and deployment along the way.
